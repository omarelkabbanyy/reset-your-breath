Introduction 
Smoking is a major global public health problem associated with high
morbidity and mortality.
Tobacco products include cigarettes, e-cigarettes (vapes),
IQOS/heated tobacco, hookah, and smokeless tobacco.
All forms of smoking can cause nicotine addiction and serious
diseases affecting the heart and lungs.
Smoking increases the risk of cancer and premature death.
Smoking and vaping are increasingly common among
      adolescents and young adults.
Both active and passive smoking negatively affect health.
Smoking is a chronic relapsing disease, making smoking cessation
essential for prevention and treatment.

Reversibility of Smoking Damage & Benefits of
Cessation:
1. Reversible Damage
• Cardiovascular function
 • Circulation improves due to recovery of endothelial function.
• Blood oxygen levels
•  Carbon monoxide is rapidly cleared from the bloodstream.
• Oxygen delivery to tissues improves.
• Airway function
• Ciliary activity recovers, improving airway clearance.
• Reduced mucus production and airway irritation.


--- PAGE 16 ---
2. Irreversible Damage:
• COPD
• Includes emphysema and chronic bronchitis.
• Smoking cessation improves symptoms and slows disease progression.
• Structural lung damage, especially emphysematous destruction of alveoli, is permanent.
• Emphysema
• Permanent destruction of alveolar walls.
• Reduced gas-exchange surface area.
• Structural lung damage
• Airway remodeling and fibrosis (scarring).
 • Persistent airflow limitation.
• DNA mutations
 • Smoking-induced genetic mutations can persist after cessation.
• Epigenetic changes
 • Some long-term alterations in gene expression remain after quitting.


--- PAGE 17 ---
Benefits of Smoking Cessation:
• Improves cardiovascular and respiratory function.
• Slows progression of smoking-related diseases.
• Reduces risk of coronary heart disease, stroke,
COPD exacerbations, and lung cancer.
• Improves quality of life and exercise tolerance.
• Increases life expectancy.
• Reduces overall morbidity and mortality.

Clinical Insight & prognosis
Clinical insights 
• Smoking is the leading preventable cause of disease and death worldwide.
•Smoking-related diseases involve multiple organ systems
simultaneously.
•Early smoking cessation significantly improve clinical
outcomes.
Prognosis 
•Smokers lose approximately 10 years of life expectancy compared with
non-smokers.
•Smoking-relate diseases are often chronic and progressive 
•Continued smoking worsens outcomes in COPD , cardiovascular disease,and
cancer
•Prognosis improves substantially after smoking cessation, regardless of
age
