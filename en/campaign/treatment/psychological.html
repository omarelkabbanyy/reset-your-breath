Psychological Treatment

Medical TreatmentNicotine Replacement TherapyPsychological TreatmentMOHP Role
Method 1

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps smokers identify triggers, change negative thoughts linked to cigarettes, and learn coping strategies instead of smoking.

Example: Instead of thinking "Smoking relaxes me," the smoker learns other relaxation methods like exercise or breathing techniques.

Method 2

Behavioral Therapy

Focuses on changing smoking habits through reward systems, habit replacement, avoiding triggers, and creating daily non-smoking routines.

Example: Chewing gum or drinking water whenever cravings appear.

Method 3

Motivational Interviewing

A counselor helps increase motivation to quit, resolve doubts and fears, and build confidence. Useful for smokers unsure about quitting.

Method 4

Support Groups and Group Therapy

Group sessions help by sharing experiences, receiving emotional support, learning from success stories, and reducing isolation.

Method 5

Stress Management Techniques

Teaches deep breathing, meditation, mindfulness, time management, and physical activity to reduce the urge to smoke during stress.

Method 6

Relapse Prevention Therapy

Helps recognize high-risk situations, prepare for cravings, recover quickly if relapse happens, and maintain long-term abstinence.

Method 7

Family and Social Support

Encouragement from family and friends improves quitting success by providing emotional support, reducing exposure to smoking environments, and increasing accountability.
